What is PMP Certification?

Candidates that are proficient in managing the people, procedures, and business priorities of professional projects are recognized by the PMP. The premier organization worldwide for project management, PMI, established the PMP in order to honor project managers who have demonstrated that they possess project leadership experience and proficiency in any area of work.
In order to become certified as a PMP, a project manager needs to fulfill specific requirements and then pass a 180-question exam. Since the PMP exam was developed by project leaders for project leaders, there is a possibility that any question on it may be connected to actual project management experiences.
Arabic, Chinese (Simplified + Traditional), English, French, German, Hebrew, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Polish (Poland), Portuguese (Brazilian), Russian, Spanish (Mexico + Spain), and Turkish are the languages in which the PMP Exam is available.
PMP certification validates that you are highly skilled in:
  • inspiring individuals and groups to succeed in every stage of a project.
  • utilizing hybrid, agile, and predictive methodologies to assess which working style is most appropriate for each project
  • highlighting a project's accomplishments and how they affect the organization's overarching strategic goals.

PMP holders earn a median salary that is 32% more in the USA and 32% higher globally than those who do not hold the certification. Source: The twelfth edition of Earning Power: Project Management Salary Survey .

Am I Eligible for the PMP Exam?

Obtaining your PMP certification requires dedication. That's one of its main selling points. Should you possess practical project management expertise, the most challenging aspect of the task is over. Make sure you meet one of the following sets of PMP Certification requirements before submitting an application:
  • College or University Degree in Four Years
  • 36 months of project management experience over the previous eight years
  • 35 hours of instruction or training in project management, or CAPM® certification

— or —

  • Diploma from High School or Secondary School
  • 60 months of project management experience over the previous eight years
  • 35 hours of instruction or training in project management, or CAPM® certification

Not Eligible?

You may qualify to apply for the CAPM certification..
